Understanding Sexual Consent

What is Consent?

Consent is a voluntary, informed, enthusiastic, and mutual agreement between people to participate in specific sexual activities. It must be given freely, without manipulation, pressure, or influence.

Key Principles of Consent

  • Consent must be clearly communicated.
  • It can be revoked at any time.
  • Silence or lack of resistance does not equal consent.
  • Consent is invalid if someone is intoxicated, unconscious, sleeping, or otherwise not capable of making sound decisions.
  • Consent applies to each activity: Saying yes to one thing does not mean agreement to everything.

Why Consent Matters

  • Builds trust and respect between partners.
  • Prevents harm, coercion, and sexual violence.
  • Promotes bodily autonomy and dignity.

How To Ask for Consent

  • Ask directly and respectfully (e.g., "Is this okay with you?").
  • Listen actively to your partner’s response.
  • Respect boundaries and accept no for an answer.
  • Encourage open and ongoing communication.

Myths & Facts About Consent

  • Myth: If someone doesn’t say no, it means yes. Fact: Consent must be clearly given.
  • Myth: Consent once means consent always. Fact: Consent must be renewed for every activity, every time.
